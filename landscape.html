Physical Landscape: Habitat & Weather

     The tropical ocean habitat is fairly unique in habitat. It is defined as being ocean bodies between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn (23.5 longitudinal degrees in either direction of the equator). This includes parts of the Pacific and Atlantic oceans, as well as the majority of the Indian Ocean. It is very warm and, well, underwater. These oceans regulate weather patterns around the globe, as warm currents flow to different paces from here, determining what is warm and what is cold, giving a tropical atmosphere to much of India, for example. The temperature of these oceans usually stays above 20 degrees Celsius, with very little variation through the year. The air is typically quite warm due to this water and sun, so there is much precipitation in tropical regions, defining tropical rainforests. The water is also quite nutrient rich from the runoff from these same forests. The mixing of cool and warm currents at the edges of these oceans has also led to many tropical storms and hurricanes.
